      Discrete Mathematics

      This course serves as a foundation for understanding mathematical logic, set theory, combinatorics, graph theory, and algorithms. It is especially useful for students in computer science, data science, and mathematics programs.

      Key Topics Covered

      This course introduces students to fundamental mathematical structures and how they relate to computer science. Discrete mathematics forms the backbone of algorithms, data structures, and programming logic.

      • Logic and Proofs
        • Propositional and predicate logic
        • Logical equivalence and implications
        • Methods of proof: direct, contrapositive, contradiction, induction
      • Set Theory
        • Operations on sets and Venn diagrams
        • Cartesian products, power sets, partitions
      • Functions and Relations
        • Injective, surjective, bijective functions
        • Equivalence relations and partial orderings
      • Combinatorics
        • Permutations and combinations
        • Pigeonhole principle
        • Inclusion-exclusion principle
      • Graph Theory
        • Graphs, trees, connectivity
        • Euler and Hamiltonian paths
        • Graph coloring and applications
      • Algorithms and Complexity
        • Recurrence relations
        • Time complexity basics
        • Asymptotic notation

      Learning Outcomes

      • Understand and apply logic and proof techniques
      • Use set theory and combinatorics in problem-solving
      • Analyze graphs and apply them to real-world scenarios
      • Lay a mathematical foundation for advanced computer science studies
